# DEVELOPMENT FINANCING STRATEGY - DOMINICAN REPUBLIC

---

## CAPÍTULO 1: INTRODUCTION AND CONTEXT

### 1. ECONOMIC AND FINANCIAL CONTEXT
- **Geographical Characteristics**
  - Eastern Region: Services and Tourism
  - Cibao Region: Agricultural
  - Southern Region: Vulnerabilities and Potential

- **Economic Dynamics**
  - Average GDP growth 5%
  - Post-pandemic resilience
  - Leadership in Latin America

- **Labor Market and Inflation**
  - Unemployment stabilized at 5%
  - Inflation below the 4% target
  - Contractionary monetary policy

- **External Sector**
  - Persistent trade deficit
  - Tourism as a driver of foreign exchange
  - Logistics hub and nearshoring

- **National Planning**
  - National Development Strategy 2030
  - National Pluriannual Plan 2025-2028
  - Target DR 2036: Double GDP

- **Fiscal Sustainability**
  - Medium-Term Fiscal Framework
  - NFPS Public Debt - Target: 45.1% of GDP
  - Improvement in credit rating

### 2. STABILITY OF THE FINANCIAL SECTOR
- **Solidity Indicators**
  - Asset growth 10.4%
  - Solvency ratio 17.4%
  - Low non-performing loans of 1.4%

- **Market Structure**
  - Predominance of Multiple Banking
  - Expansion of Investment Funds
  - Pension Funds in Public Debt

- **Resilience and Risks**
  - Satisfactory stress tests
  - Shock absorption capacity
  - Macroprudential supervision

### 3. RISKS FOR DEVELOPMENT
- **Financial Risks**
  - Crisis and systemic fragility
  - Dependence on external markets
  - Exchange rate vulnerability

- **Economic Risks**
  - External demand shocks
  - Drop in remittance flows
  - Slowdown and inflation

- **Fiscal Risks**
  - Deficit and high indebtedness
  - Subsidies to the electricity sector
  - Liabilities for pensions and litigation

- **Climate Risks**
  - Recurrent natural disasters
  - Degradation of natural resources
  - Budgetary impact in reconstruction

- **Institutional Risks**
  - Instability and polarization
  - Weakness in green monitoring
  - Perception of corruption

## CAPÍTULO 2: DOMESTIC PUBLIC FINANCING

### 1. GOVERNMENT REVENUES AND EXPENDITURES
- **Planning Instruments**
  - Medium-Term Fiscal Framework (MTFF)
  - Medium-Term Expenditure Framework (MTEF)
  - National Budget

- **Revenue Structure**
  - ITBIS (51.3% of revenues)
  - ISR (30.8% of revenues)
  - External Trade (6.2%)
  - Tax Expenditure (Exemptions 4.5% of GDP)

- **Expenditure Structure**
  - Primary Expenditure (80.3%)
  - Debt Service (19.7%)
  - Social Services (Education 4% of GDP)

- **Challenge: Fiscal Pact**
  - Lack of political consensus
  - Political cost and electoral cycles
  - Citizen mistrust

### 2. PUBLIC DEBT SUSTAINABILITY
- **Strategy 2024-2028**
  - Increase domestic financing (35%)
  - Reduce debt in foreign currency (65%)
  - Extend maturity to 12.3 years

- **Fiscal Risks and Contingent Liabilities**
  - Electricity sector deficit (1.3% of GDP)
  - Natural disasters and climate
  - Pay-as-you-go pension system

- **Institutional Progress**
  - Fiscal Responsibility Law (35-24)
  - Towards Investment Grade
  - Green, Social, and Sustainable Bonds
  - Thematic Debt Swaps

### 3. NATIONAL SYSTEM OF PUBLIC INVESTMENT (SNIP)
- **Project Life Cycle**
  - Pre-investment (Feasibility)
  - Investment (Execution)
  - Operation (Ex-post)

- **Multi-criteria Prioritization**
  - Project attributes (Progress)
  - Territorial aspects (Equity)
  - Political criteria (PNSP)

- **Challenges and Reforms**
  - Long-Term Strategic Plan (10 years)
  - Integrate public trusts
  - Strengthen impact evaluation

### 4. ILLICIT FINANCIAL FLOWS (IFF)
- **National Tax Evasion**
  - ITBIS and ISR gap
  - Electronic Invoicing (Law 32-23)
  - Use of Artificial Intelligence

- **Transnational Evasion (BEPS)**
  - Transfer Pricing
  - Automatic Exchange of Information
  - Global Minimum Tax (Pillar 2)

- **Customs Evasion**
  - Technical and open smuggling
  - Non-intrusive inspection (X-rays)
  - Post-Clearance Audit

### 5. STRATEGIC PROPOSALS
- **Pillar I: Efficient Expenditure**
  - Electricity sector reform
  - Results-based financing

- **Pillar II: IFF Shield**
  - Beneficial Ownership transparency
  - Database interoperability

- **Pillar III: Sustainability and Innovation**
  - Independent Fiscal Council
  - Recurring thematic issuances

- **Pillar IV: Comprehensive Fiscal Reform**
  - Rationalize incentives
  - Broaden tax base and progressivity

- **Pillar V: SNIP Strengthening**
  - High systemic impact projects
  - Mitigation of climate risks

## CAPÍTULO 3: INTERNATIONAL PUBLIC FINANCING

### 1. INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK (SINACID)
- **Leadership:** MINPRE (VIMICI), MIREX, MHE
- **Systems:** SI-SINACID, PDA, Catalog of Offers
- **Coordination:** High-Level Roundtable, Sectoral/Territorial Roundtables

### 2. FINANCING MODALITIES
- **Non-Reimbursable:** Grants and Technical Cooperation
- **Reimbursable:** Concessional Loans and Budget Support
- **Risk Management:** Parametric Insurance and Contingent Lines

### 3. PARTNERS FOR DEVELOPMENT
- **Multilateral Organizations**
  - IDB Group: Main creditor and anchor partner
  - CABEI: Physical infrastructure and energy
  - European Union: Grants and Global Gateway
  - World Bank: Technical leadership and reforms
  - UN: Capacity development and rights

- **Bilateral Agencies**
  - France (AFD): Concessional credits and mobility
  - USA (USAID): Historical in grants
  - Japan (JICA): Technology and waste management
  - Korea (KOICA): Digital transformation and STI
  - Spain (AECID): Institutionality and water

### 4. SOUTH-SOUTH AND TRIANGULAR COOPERATION (SSyTC)
- **Vision:** Evolution towards Dual Role (Provider)
- **Technical Offer:** SISMAP, MGP, PROAMOH, English Immersion
- **Challenges:** Limited fiscal space and systematization
- **Alliances:** Costa Rica, Colombia, Guatemala

### 5. COOPERATION EFFECTIVENESS (AGCED)
- **Strength:** Planning Quality (END 0.82)
- **Challenge:** Low budgetary registration (29%)
- **New Approaches:** Kampala Principles (Private Sector)

### 6. GREEN AND CLIMATE FINANCING
- **Instruments:** Green Taxonomy, Green Bonds, SLBs
- **Structure:** MRV System and Budgetary Classifier
- **Mechanisms:** Debt swaps, Parametric insurance
- **Gap:** Need for US$ 23,719 million (NDC)

### 7. STRATEGIC PROPOSALS
- **Pillar I:** Integrated governance and SSyTC autonomy
- **Pillar II:** Blending for pre-investment and RBF (Results-Based Financing)
- **Pillar III:** Mutual reviews and shared indicators
- **Pillar IV:** Professionalization of technical diplomacy
- **Pillar V:** General Law on Climate Change and Bankable Projects

## CAPÍTULO 5: INTERNATIONAL PRIVATE FINANCING

### 1. FOREIGN DIRECT INVESTMENT (FDI)
- **Strategic Impact**
  - Balance of payments stability
  - Energy transition financing
  - Technology transfer and know-how
  - Formal employment generation

- **Distribution and Trends**
  - Regional leadership in Central America and the Caribbean
  - Sectores: Energy, Tourism, Real Estate, Free Zones
  - Origin: United States (23.4%) and Spain (20.4%)
  - Territorial concentration: Greater Santo Domingo and Altagracia

- **Quality Challenges**
  - Low local productive integration (Enclaves)
  - Dependence on imported raw materials
  - Specialized human capital gap
  - Territorial asymmetry in the Southwest macroregion

- **Strategic Proposals**
  - Implement Pillar Two (Global Minimum Tax)
  - Binding performance agreements
  - Recovery mechanisms (Clawback)
  - Differentiated territorial incentives
  - Unification of ProDominicana-CNZFE windows

### 2. INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND EXPORTS
- **Trade Balance**
  - Structural deficit in goods
  - Surplus in services (Tourism and Modern Services)
  - Duality: Free Zones vs National Regime

- **Promotion Framework**
  - National Export Promotion Plan 2020-2030
  - Modern Services Strategy (BPO, Software)
  - General Customs Law (168-21)

- **Operational Restrictions**
  - High energy and logistics costs
  - Non-tariff barriers (Sanitary/Phytosanitary)
  - Insufficient quality infrastructure (INDOCAL)

- **Trade Finance**
  - Access gap for MSMEs
  - Role of BANDEX: Credit insurance and factoring
  - Collection management in markets with restrictions

### 3. DIASPORA AND REMITTANCES
- **Economic Importance**
  - Represent 9.5% of GDP
  - Concentration in the US (81.5%)
  - Countercyclical and stable behavior

- **Market Efficiency**
  - Dominance of remittance companies (76.9%)
  - Transfer costs above SDG target (3%)
  - Rise of digital channels and Fintech

- **Financial Inclusion and Investment**
  - Consular identification for banking access
  - Recognition of remittances for mortgage credit
  - Public-diaspora co-investment model (3x1)
  - Issuance of Diaspora Bonds

### 4. IMPACT INVESTMENT AND THEMATIC DEBT
- **Impact Ecosystem**
  - Intentionality, Return, and Measurement
  - Green Taxonomy of the Dominican Republic
  - Use of Blended Finance

- **Barriers to Investment**
  - Scarcity of bankable pipeline
  - Absence of double taxation treaty with the US
  - Illiquidity of secondary equity market

- **Debt Instruments**
  - Sovereign Green Bond consolidation
  - Sustainability-Linked Bonds (SLB)
  - Blue Bond (Ocean economy)

- **Enabling**
  - Project Preparation Facility (PPF)
  - Specialized window for impact capital
  - First-loss mechanisms

### 5. STRATEGIC PROPOSALS
- **Pillar I:** FDI Policy - Quality, linkages, and institutionality
- **Pillar II:** Export competitiveness
- **Pillar III:** Financial inclusion of the diaspora
- **Pillar IV:** International Impact Investment
